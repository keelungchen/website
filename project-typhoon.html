Overview計畫概述

Typhoons are a recurrent natural disturbance in Taiwan's marine environment, but their ecological footprint varies widely across reef sites. This project tracked recovery trajectories at six reef sites following a series of typhoon impacts in 2019–2020, testing whether pre-disturbance herbivore biomass predicts the rate and trajectory of coral recovery.

颱風是台灣海洋環境中反覆發生的自然干擾，但其生態衝擊在各礁點差異懸殊。本計畫追蹤 2019–2020 年一系列颱風衝擊後，六個礁點的恢復軌跡，驗證干擾前的草食性生物量是否能預測珊瑚的恢復速率與軌跡。

Key Findings主要發現

  • Sites with higher pre-disturbance herbivorous fish biomass showed significantly faster rates of coral recovery, particularly for branching Acropora.
    干擾前草食性魚類生物量較高的礁點，珊瑚（尤其是枝狀鹿角珊瑚）的恢復速率顯著較快。
  • Structural complexity loss was the primary driver of fish assemblage changes post-typhoon, overriding direct effects of coral cover reduction.
    颱風後結構複雜度的喪失是魚類群聚變化的主要驅動因素，其影響超過了珊瑚覆蓋率降低的直接效應。
  • Two of six sites showed no net recovery over three years, likely due to recurring storm disturbance combined with chronic anthropogenic stressors.
    六個礁點中有兩個在三年內未呈現淨恢復，可能源於反覆風暴干擾與慢性人為壓力的疊加。
