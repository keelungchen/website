Overview計畫概述

Healthy reefs require sustained observation to detect gradual change against a backdrop of natural variability. This network of 12 permanent monitoring stations along the southern Taiwan coast generates a standardised, multi-year dataset on coral community composition, benthic cover, and reef fish assemblages — providing the ecological baseline needed to assess the impact of typhoons, bleaching events, and coastal development.

健康的珊瑚礁需要持續觀測才能在自然變異背景下偵測漸進式變化。這個由 12 個永久監測站組成的網絡，沿台灣南部海岸生產標準化的多年期資料集，記錄珊瑚群落組成、底棲覆蓋率與礁魚群聚，提供評估颱風、白化事件及海岸開發衝擊所需的生態基準。

Monitoring Protocol監測協定

Each station is surveyed twice annually (spring and autumn). At each visit, two trained divers complete belt transects for coral cover and fish census, deploy photo-quadrats for machine-learning based cover analysis, and record any bleaching, disease, or physical damage observed. Data are entered in standardised field sheets and cross-checked before being uploaded to a shared database.

每個監測站每年調查兩次（春季與秋季）。每次調查由兩名受訓潛水員完成帶狀樣區的珊瑚覆蓋率與魚類調查、設置相片樣方進行機器學習覆蓋率分析，並記錄所有白化、疾病或物理損傷。資料填入標準化野外記錄表，交叉核查後上傳至共享資料庫。

Preliminary Trends初步趨勢

After three survey cycles, hard coral cover shows a slight declining trend at most Kenting sites, consistent with cumulative bleaching and storm impacts recorded in 2022–2023. In contrast, Green Island stations show stable to slightly increasing coral cover, potentially reflecting lower anthropogenic pressure and more favourable thermal conditions. Herbivorous fish biomass has remained relatively stable across all sites, suggesting sustained grazing function in the absence of major recruitment changes.

經過三輪調查，大多數墾丁礁點的硬珊瑚覆蓋率呈現輕微下降趨勢，與 2022–2023 年記錄的白化和風暴累積衝擊相符。相比之下，綠島各站點的珊瑚覆蓋率穩定乃至略有增加，可能反映出較低的人為壓力和較有利的熱條件。草食性魚類生物量在所有站點均相對穩定，顯示在無重大補充量變化的情況下，放牧功能持續維持。
